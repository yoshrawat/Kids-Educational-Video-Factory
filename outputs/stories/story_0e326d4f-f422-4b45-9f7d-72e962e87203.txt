Here's a short kids animal story for the topic of sharing and kindness, split into 4 scenes with narration and image prompts.

**Scene 1: Benny's Big Share**

Narration: "Once upon a time, in a forest filled with animals, there lived a little rabbit named Benny. Benny loved to play with his friends, but he had one big problem - he never wanted to share his toys!"

Image Prompt: A picture of Benny, a little rabbit holding onto a ball tightly.

Text: "Benny's best friend, a squirrel named Squeaky, would try to take the ball away from him. But Benny wouldn't give it up."

**Scene 2: The Magic of Sharing**

Narration: "One day, Benny's mom told him that sharing was caring. She said that if he shared his toys, others might share theirs with him too!"

Image Prompt: A picture of Benny's mom holding a big basket filled with all sorts of toys and books.

Text: "Benny decided to give Squeaky the ball. To his surprise, Squeaky started to share his own toys with Benny! They played together all day, laughing and having so much fun."

**Scene 3: A Big Helping Hand**

Narration: "As they played, Benny noticed that a family of birds had lost their nest. The wind had blown it away, and the birds were too scared to build a new one."

Image Prompt: A picture of the birds' nest floating in the air, with the birds looking worried.

Text: "Benny and Squeaky decided to help the birds. They gathered sticks and twigs and built an even bigger and better nest for them!"

**Scene 4: Benny's Big Heart**

Narration: "From that day on, Benny was known as the kindest rabbit in the forest. He shared his toys with others, helped those in need, and made new friends every day."

Image Prompt: A picture of Benny, surrounded by his friends - Squeaky the squirrel, the birds, and a whole group of animals playing together.

Text: "Benny learned that sharing and kindness can make everyone's life better. And he lived happily ever after, spreading love and kindness wherever he went!"

This story is designed to be simple and easy to understand for children aged 2-8, with a focus on the importance of sharing, kindness, and helping others. The image prompts will help bring the story to life and make it more engaging for young readers!
